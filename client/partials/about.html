ORACLE CORPORATION

2013 - 2015

I began my career as a Solutions Engineer (SE) where I led discovery calls with Account Executives (AEs) with prospects, prepared POCs, and led tailored product demonstrations of HR SaaS solutions to HR Leaders and Executives. This was a pivotal moment in my Sales career by being fortunate enough to be a part in Oracle's first "Class-of" Sales Academy. I began to learn to code HTML/CSS and JavaScript in the evenings which I then applied to making custom POCs for prospects. This boosted the team's revenue and became my "aha" moment as I began to see the potential in learning to code.

ZENEFITS

2015 - 2016

After working for 2 years as a Solutions Engineer, I wanted to try something new - this became my segue into Operations and my plunge into the world of Silicon Valley startups. Joining this Unicorn Startup (as it was at the time) was an opportunity to use my Industrial Engineering degree to improve internal workflows. I did this while supporting a variety of cross-functional teams to ensure customers had an excellent experience. CODING DOJO SAN JOSE

2016 - 2017

Zenefits had begun to rebrand itself and was rebuilding itself from the ground up. Zenefits gave me a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity to experience a hyper-growth environment first hand and I made many lasting connections through the many late nights as a result of that experience. However, I really began to miss Sales Engineering...and my lack of a CS background was a barrier to entry for many of the SE roles in Silicon Valley. I knew I had to learn these skills at some point to be an effective SE so I took this opportunity to go all in to the software developer world.

The first few lines of code I had written back at Oracle had now evolved into a burning passion of mine. I took that passion and joined a coding bootcamp where students train 1000+ hours learning 3 full technology stacks including Python, Ruby on Rails, and Node. The 'Dojo' was a rigorous experience with many late nights where we "learned to learn" and troubleshoot programming issues with minimal help. In the end, we learned to build full-blown websites and web apps from scratch with both client-side and server-side technologies.
